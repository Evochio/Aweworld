To bet or not to bet

"Very well. I'll play your game and we'll see what happens." I sighed and crossed my arms. 

I was no fool, I knew this was what they wanted. This way they had several chances of winning me over. Either by me seeing the 'fun' of this game, or them physically winning the rights from me to continue their gambling.

I ofcourse had no real obligation to follow any results of this game. But once committed, what kind of captain would I be if I went back on my word.

Arn was pleased. He quickly got us setup at a table with cards at the ready.

"Alright captain. An ideal game is for four players. To make things fair and represent our respective agendas, why dont you pick a player partner up with you."

I didnt even have to look around, Ziggy who had been hovering close by was immediately at my side.

"Let me do this 'cap!"

I nodded, she was as good as any. The cadets were the only allies I could truly trust anyway.

By the time we were all seated, a minor audience had gathered. They tried to be subtle and out of the way, but none could hide their curiosity.

At the table was me, Arn, Ziggy and Keeper with his prominent and occasionally quietly gworbling gut. Raptorians evidently took a long time to process, even for Wulfs. 

"We'll be playing Ships rules then." Arn announced. This got a nod from Keeper and a glance and shrug from Ziggy.
