The game started. In many ways it was a offshoot of the very common Wulf card game where you received two random cards, bet based on their value and then everyone match their two cards up against a open set of 5 cards on the table. Best combination wins.

But in this variation, called "The Flock" the two cards you get per round is all you get. Your betting currency is fixed to 6 chips. The Matron, of which you get one chip (worth 10 points), 2 Awe Chips (5 points) and 3 Male chips (3 points). Over 4 rounds you bet minimum of one of your chips blind against the others bets, you can only use your own starting chips. The Matron chip is special, which if you decide to pitch it always counts as first bet and done openly. The other players have to then either match it, in a challenge, with their own Matron chip and hope to win with higher value of cards or be forced to ceede a Awe chip. If you do not have a Awe chip, you must match with your own Matron or you are bust and have to give all your winnings over. After the fourth round, the one holding the biggest pool of Chip bets of another player win whatever was at stake in this game.

For example, if Keeper was so confident in his cards he bet his Matron chip in round one. Ziggy challenges him with her own 10 pointer and wins, getting his Matron and the Awe cards from the other players, playing it safe.

The final crux is that betting a male chip can only win you a male chip in return.

The open stakes on the table were:

Depending on which team is the winner, Forbidden or resumed unhindered gambling for 10 points

And wether to have restrictions or not on high-stakes for positive team points.
