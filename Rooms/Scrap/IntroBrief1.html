Admiral Avolc

-“Why is the west now so important?”

 

“For long the west sea has been closed to us. Wild weather and currents of the cold North sea and a myriad of treacherous shoals in the Sea of fangs have made sailing and exploration more dangerous then we are willing to risk ship and crew.

So most of our trade and contact with the west have lain in the calmer east, with the Wulfs as expensive proxy. Their connection westward in the southern seas are safer.

 

You glance at a elaborate map hanging off the wall in the office. You wonder what point the Admiral wants to make.

 

“But times change and we must change with it or tempt the fate of the old Plains-awes. As you may well know, trade routes westwards have grown substantially over only the last decade. Mapped paths through the sea of fangs, closely guarded by Gryph trade-houses opened the way. These maps have now somehow been revealed for all, suddenly the west is no longer closed to us. Nor others who might exploit it.”
