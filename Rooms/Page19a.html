Admiral Avolc

I order a search and plot a new course which will take us past the larger and most likely nearby islands that might have caught the wreckage.

I'd send up able male flyers, but i'd be more likely to end up searching and rescuing them instead if i did. The winds are too hard out here, with sudden dangerous up and down drafts which might toss them far out of range of the ship or worse, into the water.

The search is perilous and on several occasions the lookouts up in the mast shout out warnings for course changes to avoid collisions with treacherous reefs. I have map-readers note everything down for future referances, if we're braving these dangers we might aswell make the best of it.

The wreckage in the water increase, we are close.

A loud thud, creak and long scrape has the whole crew cringing all of a sudden. We've brushed against one of the reefs. I immediately order crew to check for major damage but its quickly apparent there are no major leaks.

I clench my jaw. The ship is fast and sturdy. Im glad for the later. But at this moment i wish we were on my old smaller and maneuverable 'Barristan'.
