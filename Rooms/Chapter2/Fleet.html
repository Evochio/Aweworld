At one point we stop at an Island briefly and a scout flies out onto the ‘Defiant’ and delivers a report, no doubt updating Captain Morlo on the fleets position.
Then off we sail again, to catch up to their location. It seems the fleet stays on constant the move.

‘Swift Bastard’ is amply named. The smaller ship is light and nimble and races ahead of us quite a deal many times. They always wait for us to catch up in the end however.
Officer Nyxie is taking this as a bit of an affront and is pushing our ship hard to go full speed. There are moments that we are matching their pace, but not for long periods, we’re just too heavy in the water with all our cargo.
It seems Captain Koen knows where we’re going. It’s a jumble of island clusters that would have taken us days to investigate, but we bypass it all. It’s a good thing we have a guide.

The Fleet

The fateful day has arrived. The look-out calls out first, spotting ships, many ships. Then I see them too, a score of sails coming our way with haste.
Much of the crew pause what they’re doing to have a look as we approach the 3rd Fleet of the Admiralty, 10 warships are visible of various make. Most of them are Galley-Frigates like ours, but dispersed around the fleet are 3 imposing battleships, nearly double our size and bristling with Ballistae.
There are 2 other ships visible too, though less armed and armored. One looks like an ordinary merchant ship, just like the one we encountered before the Sea of Fangs. The other one is of similar shape, but of distinct elegant Kingdom flair.
We’re finally here. Our objective to find them and get their report can finally be completed. Though I have mixed feelings about returning home after we’re done here.

He departs into the night. I do not get much sleep that night.

I shake my head to Stroke. Getting involved in this seems like a bad play. I’ve just arrived, I must feel the waters first, perhaps meet this Captain Klippa in the upcoming Captains-Meeting.
Stroke nods solemnly and shuffles backwards anxiously.
“Will… you turn us in? K-klippa will say she does not know me if you do. All we want is… to get home. “ Stroke fidgeted with his paws. He was right, if I tried to report they approached me, it is likely Stroke would be made to take the fall.
“I won’t do anything right now.” I reassured him. “You may leave.”
He departs into the night. I do not get much sleep that night.

I am not yet fully aware of the system of scout messengers and rendezvous points the fleet has in use, but one by one a ship or two trickles in to join the fleet until we are 15 ships all in all. 12 of these are Awe warships in various states of readiness, 2 are ordinary trade-ships and the last one a Pirate Corsair ship.
The summons to join the Admiral for a briefing upon the Battleship ‘Unwavering’ follows promptly.
The table is surrounded by a mottley assembly of captains. Being the newest face, I receive a fair share of appraising looks from those I have not already met. There are not much time for personal introductions or socializing as the Fleet-Admiral gets the briefing started in a hasty no-nonsense attitude. The Fleet Admiral stands at the head of the table, flanked to her right by the Flag-Captain and to her left by what I assume is a Soft-Spoken priestess.

“Captains. As you may have heard, Captain Avolc of the ‘Attentive’ arrived recently from the Admiralty. The news is that there are no reinforcements coming and that our requests and reports have not made it through.” She cut straight to the chase.
“All of them…?” A captain spoke up who’s name I did not know. She received a hard glare from the Fleet-Admiral.
“The ones that mattered.” The Fleet Admiral responded before continuing. “Now, I have heard all your arguments and I have listened, we have been performing a extended delaying action with the hostile elements as we waited for news and gathered intelligence. Now, however, is the time to act.”
The room seemed to hold its breath as the Fleet Admiral unveiled a map with arrows on the wall behind her. I quickly recognize it as a battle-plan.
“Tomorrow we go on the offence again. I have been studying the reports and our previous encounters and composed a plan that…”
The Fleet-Admiral is suddenly interrupted by one of the Captain thumping the table to get attention while standing taller. She has a look of slight dismay to her.
“Admiral! I apologize, but…”
“SILENCE!” The Fleet-Admiral roared, making every Awe in the crowded room raise their hackles. The very air itself obeyed and became still, so did the bustle from around the ship beyond the room.
“Captain Klippa! I am laying out the plans for our next course of action, I have not opened the table to any discussion on the matter!” She glared and leaned forward, making the Captain in question shrink back so much she might climb into the pouch of the captain next to her to escape.
“Understood!?” She looked to us all and there were a mix of nods and salutes all around. Then she continued where she left off.
“The plan is to perform a series of feints against the pursuing fleet to ensure they assemble and regroup into full strength, then to make a head-on charge as I have outlayed here.” She pointed to the battle-plan. I blinked at the numbers of ships, they were twice our number, this looked like an insane plan. No one dared speak up.
“Their numbers will work against them, we have all observed they have little coordination or discipline, relying on their superior weapons. We will deliver a decisive blow and defeat them, but only in a massed engagement that plays to our strengths rather than skirmishes of attrition.“
The Fleet-Admiral continued, further outlying the plan and took no questions or opinions upon it. It was bold, detailed and all or nothing and her will was absolute.

The interesting highlights of the meeting were about the non-combatants and their responsibility. It was the first time I got a good look at the non-Admiralty captains at the table.
A male Awe, with fancy clothing and a wulfish grin had stayed silent. In the end he was the one that dared speak up first. He was not of the Admiralty, he had a pin of the Merchant-League on his coat. A conscripted Trader as I came to understand, Captain Cholik.
“Good Admiral. I can see that you have not outlined any participation for the Merchant leagues meager ability to contribute in this battle. I assume however you do not wish us to be idle?”
“Yours and that of our Kingdom ally is to provide support post-battle and carry excess supplies.” The Admiral replied curtly.
“Mules and medical, understood Fleet-Admiral.” Captain Cholik replied. A red and black feathered Kingdom Gryph who’d sat quietly at the corner and watched the proceedings gave a courtful bow of his beak.
“House Feather-Frond will continue to assist in this endeavour.” He said with a musical lilt of a native Trade-Speech speaker.
“Captain Koen, I expect your Corsairs to participate more actively in the battle, you have been amply paid after all. You will be provided with Fire-flasks, I have put you on the east flank with the other two battleships.” The Admiral gave the Islander Captain a sparse look.
“As you wish Admiral! We’ll play to our strengths and not come up short of your fine ships!” The heavy set Islander Captain smirked with good humor.
And with that, the meeting concluded.
