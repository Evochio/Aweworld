The Mine

Weather has been tempramental for the past days, sudden squalls during the day which have kept the sailing crew on edge and regularly adjusting our sails. During the nights the wanderers in the sky have been obscured by thick overcast while the boat has been rocking heavily from waves.

At the open sea, nights in darkness are less fretful, but here among such myriad of isles and shoals there is always the niggling worry of being roused awake by an ominous scrape and shudder of the ship. It is slow going at times like these, some nights it’s better to find anchor and remain in place.

Our navigator keeps sailing us true however, shortly after dawn the Isles we seek come into sight. A larger isle then one we have encountered before, but all grey and black with barren rock and scant life clinging to it. On this side, the island looks like a tall mound, but not too steep for climbing.
Anchored to the base of the island at what looks like a crude dock is a ship. None of our make. Broad and stunty, it appears more of a tradeship for cargo.
