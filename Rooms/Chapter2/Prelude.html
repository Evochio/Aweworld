Prelude to Battle

I am gathered with my officers back at the 'Attentive' a quarter of a day later from the Captains meeting. The elation of finding the fleet has slowly been replaced with a general apprehension visible in all their eyes. As I deliver the Fleet-Admirals plans for us and the Fleet, even more so.

We’ll be part of the general formation. The Admirals plan is to draw the enemy fleet into a close chase, then trick them and charge them. We’ll be performing some preordained maneuvers before the leash comes off.
“We fought one of these ships with the Thunder-weapon and now we’re to face a whole fleet? We’ll be torn to shreds!” Officer Tick complained.
“Not necessarily.” I answered and showed a sketch of the ship profiles we’ll be facing. “The Fleet has met and assessed the strength of these ships in their previous engagements.”
I pointed to the 4 profiles of ship types we might meet, the last one on the page was familiar.
“We have the assortment of smaller ship types, dubbed ‘Small-craft’. They are fragile but quick and make up half the fleet.” I pointed to the small ships.
“Then we have the Galleons. Large captured cargo ships. They are slow and sturdy and the pirates use them mostly for boarding action. We need to avoid tangling with these, they have significant numbers of crew on them. There are only a handful however.”
“Then we have the frigates. These are a mix of captured larger tradeships and war-vessels. There are up to 10 of these.” I explained.
The crew looked a bit deflated. So I gave them the ‘good news’.
“Of all these ships, it’s only the last 4th variant, the Three-Masted Frigate, that has the Thunder-weapons able to significantly damage our ships. The rest appear to have some smaller variants that, although able to cause damage, needs a much clearer and closer shot to do so significantly.
“Ha! Give it to the Admiralty to build Warships tough enough to withstand even weapons we haven't seen before!” Officer Ulv laughed and slapped the table.
“Don't be complacent, you heard the captain, they can still damage us! And they’re over twice our number! How many of these ‘Three-Masts’ are there?” Officer Tick asked.
“That’s the good news, only two or three. But as you know, they are not to be underestimated, it only took 3 salvoes to cripple the ‘Herald’.”
“The profile looks familiar.” Officer Nyxie rubbed her chin.
“We fought one before.” I replied. “Around Pale-Rock.”
“Well.. at least it’s not 30 of those then!” Officer Keeper grinned widely, ever the optimist.

1st Force, the main attacking force.
2nd Force, the defensive force and secondary attack.
Finally the Support force, damage control, carrying wounded below deck and in charge of our special equipment.
